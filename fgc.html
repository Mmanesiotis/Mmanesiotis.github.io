About Florida Gulf Coast University

Florida Gulf Coast is an above-average public university located in Florida. It is a large institution with an enrollment of 11,098 undergraduate students. Admissions is somewhat competitive as the Florida Gulf Coast acceptance rate is 77%. Popular majors include Liberal Arts and Humanities, Psychology, and Communications. Graduating 53% of students, Florida Gulf Coast alumni go on to earn a starting salary of $35,200.

Pros

  • FGCU offers a PGM program. FGCU PGM students have the opportunity to work part-time, intern, and play at some of the most exclusive facilities in the country. Students benefit from 300+ local PGA professionals, many of which, provide formal mentorship and lend their expertise in the classroom.
  • The PGM program has a very structured schedule. Since the PGM program is in the school of business I have the opportunity to double major. Therefore I would not be handcuffed into working in the golf industry.
  • Being in the PGM program you need a car to be able to get to some of the courses and classes. This would also make it very convenient to be able to travel back home since there is an airport in Sarasota where my family owns a house and the Sarasota airport offers direct flight to Pittsburgh.

Cons

  • The FGCU only takes 50 students a year into their program. Whith that said, it makes for a competitive major. Therefore I would have to apply early decission to garentee my stop in the program.
  • The undergrand emrollment is only 11,000. Allthough that is still a large school, capared to my other schools I am interested in this is relativly small.
  • Most students claim that there really is not much to do in the surrounding areas. When new students come to the school from a bigger city, they are bored and always want to know what they can do in the area. Ft.
